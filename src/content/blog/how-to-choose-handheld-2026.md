## Step 2: Set Your Budget üí∏

Handhelds in 2026 fall into four price brackets:
*   **Budget ($50 - $150):** Mostly retro devices and older Android-based handhelds. Great for emulation.
*   **Mid-Range ($200 - $400):** This is the sweet spot for the **Switch 2** and high-end emulation devices like the **Retroid Pocket 5**.
*   **Enthusiast ($500 - $700):** The home of the **Steam Deck OLED** and entry-level Windows handhelds.
*   **Premium ($800 - $1,100):** Powerhouses like the **MSI Claw 8 AI+** or the **ROG Ally X**.

## Step 3: Ergonomics and Portability üìè

Where are you going to play?
*   **In Bed / On the Couch:** Weight doesn't matter as much. Go for the **Legion Go 2** with its massive 8.8-inch screen.
*   **On the Commute (Bus/Train):** You want something like the **ROG Ally X** or **Switch 2**. They offer a good balance of size and power.
*   **Ultra-Portable (Pocketable):** The **Anbernic RG35XX SP** or **Miyoo Mini** are the only ones that actually fit in a standard pocket.

Don't believe every marketing claim.

*   If battery is your #1 concern, the **ROG Ally X** (80Wh battery) is currently the undisputed king of the Windows world.
