The handheld PC war is over... or is it?
In the red corner, the **Steam Deck OLED**. The reigning champion of usability.
In the blue corner, the **Lenovo Legion Go 2**. The challenger that finally fixed the original's flaws.

## The Screens: OLED vs VRR LCD üì∫

*   **Steam Deck:** The OLED blacks are unbeatable. In dark games like *Dead Space*, it's immersive as hell.
*   **Legion Go 2:** Lenovo ditched the portrait tablet screen. The new **8-inch landscape VRR (Variable Refresh Rate)** panel is a game changer. Drops to 45fps feel smooth thanks to FreeSync.

**Winner:** Tie. Do you want contrast (Deck) or smoothness (Legion)?

The original Legion Go was a brick. The Go 2 is lighter (approx 680g), but the Steam Deck OLED still hugs your hands better.
However, Lenovo's **detachable controllers** (FPS Mode) are still a cool party trick, even if I rarely use them.

## Software: SteamOS vs Windows 11 (Debloated) üíª

This is where the battle is fought.
*   **Steam Deck:** It just works. Resume game instantly. No drivers to update manually.
*   **Legion Go 2:** Lenovo's new "Legion Space" overlay is much better, hiding Windows almost completely. But it's still Windows. You *will* have to deal with updates eventually.

## The Verdict üèÜ

**Buy the Steam Deck OLED if:** You want a console experience. You hate tinkering. You value battery life (still 20% better).

**Buy the Legion Go 2 if:** You play Game Pass (native support), want a bigger screen, or need raw power (Z2 Extreme chip) for newer AAA games.
